    A Growing Economy

    • CBD and Hemp Industry:
      • Among the best growing conditions in the country for hemp according to industry experts
      • Startup CBD Biosciences building nation's largest hemp factory in Pueblo
    • Economic Opportunity:
      • PuebloPlex mixed-use industrial space will provide between 10k and 25k jobs
      • Green chili market in Pueblo expected to grow 9.4% annually




"The potential economic impact [of CBD and hemp] could be extremely dramatic to the benefit of Pueblo.”


-Chris Markuson, Pueblo County Economic Development Director

    A Growing Economy

    • A Haven for Business:
      • Small businesses have identified the Pueblo community as a deciding factor in setting up shop in Pueblo
      • Solar Roast Coffee started as an individual shop in Pueblo - now sells to 315 retailers nationwide




“We could be based anywhere in the world and do quite well. We chose, and still choose, Pueblo.”


- Ryan McWilliams, CEO International Engineering

    An Investment in the Future

    • Renewable Energy:
      • February 2017 resolution by Pueblo City Council to move the city to 100% renewable energy by 2035
      • Xcel Energy is investing $2.5 billion in clean energy for Pueblo and rural Colorado
    • Economic Impact:
      • Home to Comanche Solar Project - the largest solar panel farm east of the Rockies
      • Vestas wind turbine production company, based in Pueblo, is the largest tower factory in the world





“Once a city of smokestacks, Pueblo's future looks a lot sunnier”


- Colorado Politics, December 2017

    A Desirable Destination

    • Tourism in Pueblo:
      • Historic Arkansas River Walk of Pueblo
      • Colorado State Fair
      • Colorado Chile & Frijoles Festival
      • Black Iron Building Retail and Restaurants coming soon
    • Arts and Culture:
      • Pueblo Levee Mural Project - world's largest continuous painting
      • Pueblo Creative Corridor - a Colorado Certified Creative District
      • Burgeoning arts scene




“I’ve always had this vision that this could turn into an eye-candy city, with murals everywhere, and we have that potential now. Now I’m seeing it as the mural capital of the world”


- Tom Latka, owner Latka Studio

    A Transit Hub

    • Virgin Hyperloop One:
      • Route will travel directly between Denver and Pueblo
      • 17 minute commute time (vs two hours by car)
      • Anticipated operational in mid-2020s
    • EVRAZ Steel Mill to Expand in Pueblo:
      • EVRAZ North America will produce 11,000 tons of steel to upgrade tracks for Southwest Chief Amtrack line
      • EVRAZ committed to keeping a workforce of at least 1,000 full-time employees at the mill earning an average pre-benefit wage of $60,000 to $65,000 per year
    • Black Iron Building will be "one of the preeminent railway technology centers in the world."




“Something of this [Steel Mill Expansion's] magnitude, we haven’t seen for 50 to 60 years"


- Jeff Shaw, President and CEO of Pueblo Economic Development Corp.
